About the tokens

Since 2012, Twitter's API (an interface for external applications like ours) requires user authentication and limits the amount of requests per application and minute. To request the API more frequently, we need Access Tokens that are generated upon login. Every user, who logs in through our website generates a token. We need approximately 600 of them to collect the German data and multiple thousands to scale up to Twitter's entire network. That's why your help is decisive!

Privacy

If you authenticate with your Twitter account, we cannot see or read your private data. We chose the most restrictive permission to your data, which is read-only. This will allow our application to read Tweets on your timeline and see who you follow. In general, this is public information, as Twitter is an open social network.

If you manually changed your privacy-settings to hide who you follow or to hide your tweets, we will be able to see this information after you authenticated.
